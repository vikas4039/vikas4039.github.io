Liquid Limit Test

Home Apparatus Theory Observation Result


Procedure

1. About 200 gm of air-dried soil from thoroughly mixed portion of material passing 425 micron I.S sieve is to be obtained.


2. Weight of the empty containers is measured.


3. Mix the sample thoroughly with distilled water in an evaporating dish or a glass plate to form a uniform paste.


4. A portion of the paste is placed in the cup of Casagrende's device and spread into portion with few strokes of spatula.
    Trim it to a depth of 1cm at the point of maximum thickness and return excess of soil to the dish.


5. Cut a groove in the sample in the cup by using the grooving tool. Draw the grooving tool through the paste in the cup
     along the diameter, through the centre line of the cup. Hold the tool perpendicular to the cup.


6. Turn the handle of the device at a rate of 2 revolutions per second. Count the number of blows until the two halves of
     the soil specimen come in contact due to flow and not by sliding.


7. The number of blows required to cause the groove close shall be recorded.

8. A representative portion of soil is taken from the cup for water content determination and weight is measured.

9. Repeat the test with different moisture contents at least five more times for blows between 10 and 35.
