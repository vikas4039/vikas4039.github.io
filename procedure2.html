Procedure

1. About 200 gm of air-dried soil from thoroughly mixed portion of material passing 425 micron I.S sieve is to be obtained.
>


2. Weight of the empty containers is measured.


3. Mix the sample thoroughly with distilled water in an evaporating dish or a glass plate to form a uniform paste.


4. Then the wet soil paste is transferred to the cylindrical cup of cone penetrometer apparatus, ensuring that no air is
trapped in this process.


5. Finally the wet soil is leveled up to the top of the cup and placed on the base of the cone penetrometer apparatus.


6. The penetrometer is so adjusted that the cone point just touches the surface of the soil paste in the cup and the
initial ready is to be taken.


7. The vertical clamp is then released allowing the cone to penetrate into soil paste under its own weight for 5 seconds.
After 5 seconds the penetration of the cone is noted to the nearest millimeter.


8. The test is repeated at least to have four sets of values of penetration in the range of 14 to 28 mm.

9. The exact moisture content of each trial is determined.
